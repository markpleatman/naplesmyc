Welcome to NMYC

About Us

The Naples Model Yacht Club races five classes of radio-controlled sailboats at North Collier Regional Park. Our season runs from January through March, with off-season racing throughout the year.

Whether you're a seasoned skipper or brand new to RC sailing, NMYC offers a welcoming, competitive, and fun environment on the water.

We are affiliated with US Sailing and race under the Racing Rules of Sailing.

Code of Conduct

  1. Learn and sail by the Racing Rules of Sailing (RRS)
  2. Always call infractions: hail twice "66 protests 55"
  3. Acknowledge protests and perform penalty turns promptly
  4. Follow the Corinthian Spirit — do penalty turns even when not protested
  5. Refrain from derogatory or demeaning remarks; be friendly and courteous
  6. Help new or less skilled members with technique and RRS
  7. Be a good sportsman — this is a fun sport

Classes We Race

5 Fleets

Soling 1 Meter

The largest sanctioned AMYA class with over 3,100 registered boats. NMYC has 50+ sailors in this class. Inexpensive, easy to sail. LOA 39.38", 592 sq in sail area, 10" draft, 10 lb minimum weight.

Fleet Captain: Tom Paytosh · Off-season: Richard Flach

DragonFlite 95

Arguably the fastest-growing class in the U.S. NMYC's DF95 fleet has 45+ sailors and alternates scored and fun races. Arrives 90% ready to race — just rig, add batteries, and bind your receiver.

Fleet Captains: Dick Hedderick (Red) · TBD (White)

DragonForce 65

The little sibling of the DF95 — same go-fast features including carbon fiber keel, drum winch, and room for a four-pack LiFe battery. A great entry point into competitive RC sailing.

Fleet Captain: Hank Sykes · Off-season: Sam Westbladh

SeaWind

KYOSHO's One Design class. 39.3" LOA, 8.9" beam, 6.5 lb minimum weight, 604 sq in sail area. A great one-design racing experience.

Contact: Chuck Weaver

IOM — International One Meter

The newest fleet at NMYC, growing to twelve active boats in its first three months in 2023. The IOM requires a more significant financial commitment and appeals to sailors with deep RRS knowledge. Learn more at iomusa.org.

Fleet Captain: Ron Stryker

Location & Parking

Where to Find Us

Pond #1 — Primary Venue

North Collier Regional Park
15000 Livingston Rd, Naples, FL (just south of Immokalee Rd)

After entering the park, turn right at the first access road. Park in the designated spaces south of the Sun 'n Fun Water Park. The area accommodates approximately 15 cars — park close to your neighbor. With 13 buoys, courses can be set to match any wind condition.
